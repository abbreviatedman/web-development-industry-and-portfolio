# Web Development Industry and Portfolio

### Course Overview

This 4-day course has three main objectives:

1. To provide an overview of the web development industry.
2. To prepare the student for job interviews in the tech field.
3. To guide the student as they start building a portfolio to showcase their work.

##### The Web Development Industry

The web development industry is a fast-paced and ever-changing field. It is important to stay up-to-date with the latest technologies and trends. We will be learning how you can integrate into that industry, including the roles and responsibilities of web developers, the skills and tools needed to succeed in the field, and the latest trends and technologies in web development.

##### Job Interviews

Job interviews in the tech field can be challenging. It is important to be prepared and to practice your interview skills. This course will provide tips and strategies for preparing for job interviews, as well as sample interview questions and answers.

##### Portfolio Building

Building a portfolio is an essential part of the job search process. Your portfolio is a showcase of your work and skills, and can help you stand out from other candidates. This course will provide guidance on how to build a portfolio, including what to include and how to present your work effectively.

### Portfolio

You will work on one main project in this course: your web developer portfolio. 

##### Portfolio Guide

The student's portfolio should have the following:

1. A professional-looking design.
2. A brief introduction about yourself.
3. A list of your skills and experience.
4. A showcase of your projects.
5. Relevant links.

Let's walk through each of these sections in more detail.

##### Professional Design

Your portfolio should have a professional-looking design. This means using a clean and modern layout, with a color scheme that is easy on the eyes. You should also use high-quality images and graphics to make your portfolio visually appealing.

Many web developers struggle with designing their portfolio, as many of us are do not have a visual design background. Here are some tips to help you design your portfolio.

**What Makes A Good Portfolio Design?**

1. Clean and modern layout. A clean and modern layout is easy to read and navigate. It should be well-organized and visually appealing.
2. Color scheme. You should use colors that complement each other and make your portfolio look professional.
3. High-quality images and graphics. High-quality images and graphics can make your portfolio visually appealing. You should use images that are clear and well-designed.
4. Consistent design. You should use the same fonts, colors, and layout throughout your portfolio to create a cohesive look.
5. Responsive design. Your portfolio should be accessible on all devices, including desktops, tablets, and smartphones.
6. Easy navigation. Your portfolio should be easy to navigate, with clear links and buttons that make it easy for users to find what they are looking for.
7. Contact information. You should include your email address, phone number, and any other contact information you want to share with potential employers.
8. Call to action. You should include a call to action that encourages users to contact you or view your projects.
9. Social media links. You should include links to your LinkedIn profile, GitHub profile, and any other online profiles you want to showcase.
10. About me section. You should include a brief introduction about yourself, including your background, education, and career goals.

##### Introduction

Your portfolio should include a brief introduction about yourself. This can include information about your background, education, and career goals. You can also include a photo of yourself to personalize your portfolio.

##### Skills and Experience

Your portfolio should include a list of your skills and experience. This can include your technical skills, such as programming languages and frameworks, as well as any relevant work experience or projects you have completed.

##### Showcase of Projects

The most important part of your portfolio is the showcase of your projects. This is where you can demonstrate your skills and experience to potential employers. You should include a variety of projects, such as websites, web applications, and other coding projects.

##### Relevant Links

Your portfolio should include relevant links, such as links to your GitHub profile, LinkedIn profile, and any other online profiles or projects you want to showcase. You should also include contact information so potential employers can reach out to you.

### Web Development Industry

We'll learn about the web development industry, including the different roles and responsibilities of web developers, the skills and tools needed to succeed in the field, and the latest trends and technologies in web development.

##### Roles and Responsibilities

There are many different roles and responsibilities in the web development industry.

**Technical Roles**:

1. Front-end developer: Front-end developers are responsible for creating the user interface and user experience of a website or web application. They use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create interactive and responsive web pages.
2. Back-end developer: Back-end developers are responsible for the server-side logic of a website or web application. They use JavaScript or another programming languages such as Python, Ruby, or Java to build the back-end infrastructure that powers the front-end user interface.
3. Full-stack developer: Full-stack developers are responsible for both the front-end and back-end development of a website or web application. They have a broad range of skills and can work on all aspects of a project. **This role is the most common in the industry**, since having someone who can work on both the front-end and back-end of a project gives the company more value for their money.
4. DevOps engineer: DevOps engineers are responsible for the deployment and maintenance of a website or web application. They work with developers and IT operations teams to automate the deployment process and ensure that the project is running smoothly.

**Non-Technical Roles**:

1. Web designer: Web designers are responsible for the visual design of a website or web application. They use graphic design tools such as Adobe Photoshop or Sketch to create the layout, color scheme, and typography of a website.
2. UI/UX designer: UI/UX designers are responsible for the user interface and user experience of a website or web application. They use design principles and user research to create a website that is easy to use and visually appealing.
3. Product manager: Product managers are responsible for the overall strategy and direction of a website or web application. They work with developers, designers, and other team members to define the goals and objectives of a project.
4. Project manager: Project managers are responsible for managing the timeline, budget, and resources of a website or web application. They work with developers, designers, and other team members to ensure that the project is completed on time and within budget.
5. Quality assurance (QA) engineer: QA engineers are responsible for testing a website or web application to ensure that it works correctly and meets the requirements. They use automated testing tools and manual testing techniques to identify bugs and issues in a project.

##### Skills and Tools

There are many skills and tools that are needed to succeed in the web development industry.

**Technical Skills**:

1. HTML: HTML is the standard markup language for creating web pages and web applications. It is used to structure the content of a website and define the layout of a web page.
2. CSS: CSS is the style sheet language for designing the visual presentation of a website or web application. It is used to style the layout, color, and typography of a web page.
3. JavaScript: JavaScript is the programming language for creating interactive and dynamic web pages. It is used to add interactivity, animations, and other dynamic elements to a website.
4. Front-end frameworks: Front-end frameworks such as React, Angular, and Vue.js are used to build interactive and responsive web applications. They provide pre-built components and tools to speed up the development process.
5. Back-end frameworks: Back-end frameworks such as Node.js, Django, and Ruby on Rails are used to build the server-side logic of a website or web application. They provide tools and libraries to handle data storage, authentication, and other back-end tasks.
6. Databases: Databases such as MySQL, PostgreSQL, and MongoDB are used to store and retrieve data in a website or web application. They provide a way to store and manage data in a structured format.
7. Version control: Version control systems such as Git are used to track changes to a project and collaborate with other developers. They provide a way to manage and share code in a team environment.
8. Testing: Testing tools such as Jest, Mocha, and Selenium are used to test a website or web application to ensure that it works correctly and meets the requirements. They provide a way to automate testing and identify bugs and issues in a project.
9. Deployment: Deployment tools such as Heroku, Netlify, and AWS are used to deploy a website or web application to a server or hosting platform. They provide a way to publish a project and make it accessible to users.
10. DevOps: DevOps tools such as Docker, Kubernetes, and Jenkins are used to automate the deployment and maintenance of a website or web application. They provide a way to streamline the development process and ensure that a project is running smoothly.

### Interview Preparation

Acing the technical interview requires practice and preparation. This course will provide tips and strategies for preparing for job interviews, as well as providing guided practice with sample interview questions and answers.


##### Interview Tips

Here are some tips to help you prepare for your technical interview:

1. Research the company: Make sure you know about the company and its products or services.
2. Practice coding: Make sure you are comfortable writing code on a whiteboard or in an online coding environment.
3. Review your technical skills: Make sure you are familiar with the technical skills required for the job.
4. Prepare for behavioral questions: Be prepared to answer questions about your experience and how you work with others.
5. Ask questions: Be prepared to ask questions about the company and the job.
6. Be confident: Be confident in your abilities and be prepared to talk about your experience and skills.
7. Practice: Practice answering sample interview questions and coding problems.
8. Get feedback: Get feedback from others on your interview performance.
9. Be prepared: Make sure you have everything you need for the interview, including a copy of your resume and any other materials you may need.
10. Relax: Try to relax and stay calm during the interview.

##### What's the technical interview like?

The technical interview is a key part of the job interview process for many tech companies. It is designed to test your technical skills and problem-solving abilities. The technical interview may include coding problems, algorithmic questions, and questions about your technical knowledge and experience.
